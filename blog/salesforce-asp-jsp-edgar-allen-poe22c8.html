Salesforce, ASP, JSP and Edgar Allen Poe

August 8, 2020 Saurabh

An Architect was asked for a website to show closing stock prices
Salesforce was ridiculed as "Roach Motel" in those days

That's easy

Client/Server ASP/JSP with SQL db

Dev team estimated a staggering 6 digit number

There has to be a better way

The architect thought

He spent the weekend

Learnt Salesforce from scratch

Created custom objects for Stock prices

Built an ASP frontend

Pulled Salesforce data via APIs

And showed Stock prices

Monday AM, he was in his VP's office demoing it

What?

Salesforce?

Why?

No!

Thanks!

Dismayed :(

He went to his colleague, his Yoda

"Do you need a Website or a Web App?" said Yoda

Umm?

"A nightly script can do static HTML pages on a web server"

"You need market close data, not real-time. Correct?"

In all the ASP, JSP, Salesforce options

He had ignored the fundamental question

What was really needed?

Most basic questions matter

And are often ignored

Because our thinking can be limited by what we learn

And hear from our peer group

Sometimes the answer is buried

Under the floorboards

On top of which a shiny technology sits

As in an Edgar Allen Poe story

Oh...and the architect went elsewhere and did other things

Remembering the allure of complex

And E.A.P.
